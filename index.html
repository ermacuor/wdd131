La Paz - Bolivia

At over 3,600 meters above sea level, La Paz, Bolivia stuns as the world’s highest capital city—dramatically carved into a canyon and guarded by the towering Illimani mountain. This high-altitude metropolis is a whirlwind of contrasts: colonial streets and futuristic cable cars, sacred indigenous rituals and buzzing nightlife, traditional cholitas and bold street art. Wandering its narrow alleys, you're as likely to encounter an ancient Aymara ceremony as you are a trendy café filled with students and travelers. But La Paz is more than just a city—it's a gateway to Bolivia’s wonders. Ride the sky-high Mi Teleférico for cinematic views, then head out to marvel at the Moon Valley’s bizarre rock formations or the legendary Lake Titicaca nearby. Whether you're drawn to its mystical Witches’ Market, the echoes of pre-Inca civilizations, or the sheer thrill of walking among the clouds, La Paz invites you to breathe deep, explore boldly, and never quite touch the ground.
